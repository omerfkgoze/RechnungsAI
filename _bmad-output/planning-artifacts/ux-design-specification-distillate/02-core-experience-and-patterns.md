## Core Experience and UX Patterns

- Defining experience: "Document Destruction" loop; capture (snap photo) then confirm (glance at AI work, approve in <2s if green); measure of success = how quickly each invoice disappears from mental load
- Core interaction: Capture then Witness magic then Approve then Forget
- Mental model shift: from "Chaos Accumulation" (physical pile, visual guilt, panic day on 10th of month, 3+ hours reactive data entry) to "Instant Destruction" (invoice destroyed on contact, never becomes a pile, Monday morning = 15-min satisfying ritual)
- Parallel pipeline: AI processing begins immediately in background while user captures next invoice; zero-wait capture; camera stays open after each photo; batch-then-review
- "Tinder swipe" approval: high-confidence green invoices approved with single swipe right; amber fields pulse gently; red require explicit action; green fields visually recessive
- Smart defaults: SKR plan from onboarding; DATEV settings configured once; export date auto-suggested from last export
- Experience principles: (1) "Invoice Dies on Contact" minimize time capture-to-resolution; (2) "Attention Is Scarce" UI never demands attention where AI confident; (3) "App Not Website" native/immediate/tactile feel; (4) "Sell Time and Trust Not Features" value in hours saved and errors prevented; (5) "Progressive Trust" builds through repeated positive experiences
- Emotional goals: dominant=Competence (user feels skilled professional not software learner); supporting=Pride/Discovery (new professional identity); supporting=Safety Net Confidence (co-pilot not autopilot)
- Emotional journey: Discovery=curiosity+relief; Onboarding=trust+safety; First Capture=wonder+competence; Review=control+mastery; Error=collaboration+agency ("AI needs your expertise" not "AI failed"); Export=relief+pride; Weekly Recap=accomplishment+validation; Return=familiarity+anticipation
- Micro-emotions: haptic feedback on approval; green checkmark animation; optional confirmation tone; milestone celebrations (non-patronizing); confidence scores always visible; user can always override AI
- Emotional design principles: (1) Competence Not Compliance; (2) Errors Are Collaboration Not Failure; (3) Every Output Source of Pride; (4) Ambient Safety Not Active Reassurance; (5) Measure and Celebrate Progress
- UX inspiration: WhatsApp=zero-step camera, implicit file management, checkmark status (captured/processing/ready/confirmed/exported); Duolingo=professional streaks (warm not playful, no mascots/XP); Tinder/iOS Mail=swipe-to-action; Mobile banking=photo-transfer trust transfer
- Competitor patterns: Lexoffice mobile OCR speed (extend to auto-detect+auto-capture); sevDesk VAT color differentiation (extend into full confidence system); DATEV output discipline (invisible backbone, mess-to-order transformation)
- Anti-patterns: (1) no modal interruptions except destructive confirmations; (2) no forced keyboard input on mobile (everything selectable/tappable/swipeable); (3) no technical error messages (conversational German, specific, actionable); (4) DATEV Export never buried in hamburger menu; (5) no feature-first onboarding (start with "take a photo")
