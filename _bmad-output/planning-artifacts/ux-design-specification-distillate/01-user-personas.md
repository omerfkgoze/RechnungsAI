## User Personas and Design Challenges

- Thomas (primary): age 40+, Handwerk business owner (carpentry/plumbing/electrical), 5-20 employees; 25-30 incoming invoices/week via Excel/Word; zero digital maturity; deep cloud data skepticism; desktop for bulk, mobile for photo capture on job sites
- Lisa (primary): age 25-35, e-commerce operator on Shopify/Amazon DE; 60+ supplier invoices/month from multiple countries; digitally savvy but overwhelmed by German accounting/VAT complexity; needs multi-format + intra-EU VAT; primarily desktop
- Frau Schmidt (indirect): Steuerberater; judges product by DATEV export quality (zero errors, perfect format); becomes organic distribution channel when impressed; future direct user via Steuerberater portal (Phase 4)
- Challenge 1: digital maturity 5/100; interface must work for someone who has never used SaaS; core workflow (upload/review/confirm) completable without help
- Challenge 2: trust deficit; conservative German tradespeople 40+ skeptical of cloud financial data; must communicate German servers, GoBD, DSGVO, bank-grade encryption at critical moments without intrusion
- Challenge 3: AI uncertainty; confidence scoring (green/amber/red) must be immediately intuitive; human-in-the-loop as natural quality check not burden
- Challenge 4: regulatory complexity invisible; Thomas never sees EN 16931, just "compliant" or "action needed"
- Opportunity 1: "aha moment" (3s extraction with cascade animation) as competitive moat and sales tool
- Opportunity 2: quantified value reinforcement via weekly notifications (invoices processed, hours saved, VAT deductions ready)
- Opportunity 3: Steuerberater as organic growth channel via flawless DATEV export
- Opportunity 4: progressive trust building through repeated positive experiences rather than front-loaded reassurance
